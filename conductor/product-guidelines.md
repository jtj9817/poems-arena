# Classicist's Sanctuary - Product Guidelines

## Brand Voice

- **Scholarly and Contemplative:** The voice is authoritative, informed, and respectful of literary traditions. It treats poetry with reverence, mimicking the tone of a high-end literary journal or museum.
- **Enigmatic and Provocative:** It embraces the mystery of the "duel," posing the central question ("human or synthetic?") without providing easy answers. It challenges the reader to trust their own perception.
- **Minimalist and Uncluttered:** Avoids marketing-speak, hype, or technical jargon. Let the text of the poems speak for itself.

## Visual Principles: "Digital Letterpress"

- **Haptic Sensation:** The interface should evoke the physical presence of paper and ink. Use subtle textures and shadows to create a sense of depth and materiality.
- **Generous Negative Space:** Provide ample room for the poems to breathe. Avoid crowding the UI with extraneous buttons, menus, or labels.
- **Restricted Palette:** Adhere strictly to the "Ink," "Paper," and "Stock" colors defined in the product guide. Accents (Seal Red, Binding Blue) should be used sparingly for critical feedback.
- **Classic Typography:** Use the specified serif faces (Piazzolla, EB Garamond) at sizes that promote readability and evoke the authoritative feel of high-quality print.

## User Experience (UX) Standards

- **Distraction-Free Focus:** In the Reading Room, eliminate all UI elements that are not essential for reading and voting. No sidebars, no ads, no navigation menus.
- **Material Interactions:** Transitions should feel smooth and natural, not robotic or "snappy." Animations should be subtle and purposeful, enhancing the haptic experience.
- **Text-First Hierarchy:** The text of the poem is always the most important element on the screen. All other elements are secondary and should not compete for attention.
